School Discipline

Discipline is part of every SFA-DLS pupil/student's way of life. The behavioural manifestation of the school's form of discipline is what identifies its pupils/students apart from the rest. Rules are set to form young people's way into the acceptable standards of the human society and to help them contribute more productively to the larger community.

The SFA Norms of Conduct ON and OFF Campus

1. School Jurisdiction

Any act committed outside the school, is considered the responsibility of parents or guardians. on the contrary , all acts committed outside the school, in representation to an official school function (such as field trips, athletic competitions, parades and the likes), are considered within the responsibility of the school authorities. therefore, the latter would warrant the prescribe school discipline.

2. Cafeteria/ canteen

The canteen is a place where discipline and respect can clearly observed. Every student is expected to form a line in proceeding to canteen counters to buy food. he/she is also expected to treat the canteen personnel(including security guard, janitors, non-teaching staff) with respect and consideration and to leave premises mindful of its cleanliness.

3. At the library

The library is a place where silence is strictly observed. Students and teachers go to the library for private reading, research and study. Therefore all library users are expected to abide its rules and regulations.

4. In the school bus

Good manners and proper decorum are always expected to every SFA student, in whatever venue of circumstances he/she maybe in. the school bus is a venue where these virtues should be observed and as such, all students availing of the school bus services are to exhibit them.

5. Morning prayer

The pupils/students must observed the following:

    a. Standing at attention during the entire ceremony.
    b. Singing the national anthem and reciting the pledge with respect
    c. Singing the alma mater song with pride.

6. General assembly

In the activities requiring the attendance of the entire school, the students are expected to:

    a. Be alert in observing all bell signals
    b. Be silent and to remain in order.
    c. Observe proper line formation

7. In classrooms and Offices

The students are expected to observe the following while inside the mentioned venues:

    a. Courtesy
    b. Silence
    c. Respect for the place and its fixtures



Offenses - Definition of Terms

A. Student Academic and Deportment (SAD) Slip.

This slip is issued to a pupil/student who incurs minor offenses despite repeated oral warnings. The parent/guardian signs the slip to indicate receipt and knowledge of incident and return it to the PREFECT OF DISCIPLINE, through their child.

Only three SAD slips, bearing the same offenses or violating will be issued to a child. the fourth, similar violating will incur violation report.

B. Violation Report

A Violation Report (VR) is a form accomplished by the subject teacher or class adviser present ay the occurrence of the offense. The form is signed by the teacher, and is submitted to the office of the prefect of discipline. the parents/guardians are sent two copies of the VR and are asked to sign one and to return the signed copy to indicate that they have received the information. Three (3) VRs of various, accumulated offenses is equivalent to an NI in conduct on the quarter when the third violation, meriting a VR, was committed. Home visitation may be conducted, in the second VR has not been returned, despite numerous oral written reminders.

Offenses - Minor Offenses

A minor offense is an offense committed without malicious, or any intention to harm. if a pupil/student commits any minor offense, the teacher present at the time who acts as a witness to the offense, will make an anecdotal record of what happened and submitted his/her report to the prefect of discipline. The prefect of discipline may impose the following:

1st offense: ORAL WARNING
2nd offense: SAD SLIP
3rd offense: VIOLATION REPORT (MS in conduct for the quarter)
4th offense: VIOLATION REPORT (conference with the parents NI in conduct)

Rules and Regulation - Attendance

Attendance is an essential part of a child's structured learning process. To company with the anticipated development and offering of skills and concept, a child has to be present in all learning encounters, promptly and regularly. Parents and guardians are expected to cooperate with this school in ensuring their children understand the connection between learning and responsibility.

Absences

Any pupil/student who is absent on a given day is still responsible for his/her learning. S/he is required to make up for any of the lesson s s/he missed, to submit all requirements assigned on the particular day, and to be ready for any quiz that may be given on the day of his/her return. If a quiz or a test is administered on the day of his absence, no make up tests will be provided. As per DepEd policy if a pupil/student has incurred accumulated absences equivalent to 20% or more of the total number of school days for the year, s/he is dropped from the official register. Note:

    1. When a pupil/student is absent for three consecutive days, a family representative must inform the school of the cause of the said absences.

    2. When pupil/student returns to school after his/her absences letter of excuse, signed by the parents/guardians and a medical certificate is presented to the class adviser. This is then forwarded to the prefect of discipline office.

    3. When a pupil/student is projected to be absent on a certain days, the parents/guardians submit letter to explain or request to the class adviser. This is likewise forwarded to the prefect of discipline office.

Tardiness (Grade 1 to Year IV)

A pupil/student who comes in late for his class is issued a LATE PASS and is marked LATE in the attendance folder. FOUR (4) occurrences of tardiness (within a month) will merit a Notice of tardiness, issued by the office of the prefect of discipline.

    1. Deportment Probation on tardiness (DPT)
    A pupil/student is placed on DPT the following school year, if he/she accumulated 20 or more occurrences of tardiness.

    2. Lifting of Deportment Probation on Tardiness
    DPT is lifted, if the pupil/student has less than 10 incident of tardiness during the year that he/she is on probation.

    3. Special Deportment Probation on Tardiness (SDPT)
    If a pupil/student in curs 10 or more incidents of tardiness on the year of probation, he/she is put on Special Deportment Probation on Tardiness the following year. During the year that the SDPT is on, the pupil/student must not incur more than 4 incidents of tardiness, otherwise s/he is asked to be withdrawn from school.

    4. Lifting of the Special Deportment Probation on Tardiness
    The SDPT is lifted at the beginning of the next school year, if the pupil/student has not incurred more than 4 incidents of tardiness in his/her probation year.

Pulling out of Class

Parents or guardians are NOT allowed to pull a pupil/student out from the class without any urgent reason, or without an OFF CAMPUS PASS issued by the Prefect of Discipline. Pupils/students can only be pulled out because of the following reason: death in the family, funeral of immediate kin (first degree) and emergency cases.

Accompanying Children to School

Parents/guardians of kinder 1,2 and grade 1 pupil/students are allowed to accompany them to the classrooms only on the FIRST DAY OF SCHOOL. After setting them in the classroom, they should leave immediately.

Rules and Regulation - Off Limits Regulation

SFA pupil/students should remain within the areas designated for them. The following is a list of off limit areas: AT ALL TIMES

1. Faculty lounges and work areas
2. Parking Areas
3. Office for Instructional Materials
4. Area beside the classroom (right window side)
5. Construction Areas

Rules and Regulation - Transportation

The school must be informed on the kind of transportation the parents/guardians have arranged for their child. For commuters, a special letter of authorization, signed by the parents/guardians would have to be submitted to the office of the Prefect of Discipline for reference purposes.

Rules and Regulation - Ambulant Vendors

Buying from ambulant vendors and stores outside the premises, within school hours is prohibited. Anyone caught engaging in suck activity will be dealt accordingly.

Rules and Regulation - Circular Letters

A circular letter is a letter addresses to the parents/guardians of all SFA pupils/students. The letter carried official announcements and messages regarding school matters. Parents/ guardians are held accountable for any information contained therein, and are required to return the reply slips (with their signature) attached to each letter, to confirmed their understanding of the school correspondence.

Rules and Regulation - Delivery of Articles

SFA wants to develop in each pupil/student a sense of responsibility and independence. This includes accountability over his/her personal needs in school. Therefore, delivery of articles, such as projects, notebooks, lunch boxes, money of similar articles, during school hours, is discouraged pupils/students should bring this with them as they come to school. Only in extreme cases such as requests granted, and this would need clearance from the office of the Prefect of Discipline.

Rules and Regulation - Lost and Found

Honesty is a strong SFA virtue. All pupils/students are encouraged to turn over any item found, to the office of Prefect of Discipline. These are then posted for claim.

All pupils. Students are likewise encouraged to label all their belongings. Any loss should be reported to the mentioned office immediately. To claim a lost item. A pupil/student must be able to correctly identify it, before it is released to him/her. Any unclaimed article shall be collected and donated to the poor, at the end of the school year. The school does not assume responsibility for the loss of the personal belongings left unattended with the loss of the personal belongings left unattended with the school premises after class hours.

Rules and Regulation - Confiscated Items

Expensive items such as cellular phones, game boy, PSP, and like are not allowed in the school. These will be confiscated and turned over to the Prefect of Discipline for safekeeping.

A letter from the parents/guardians is required for the return of the confiscated article to the owner. For every expensive items, parents/guardians are required to confer with Prefect of Discipline before the item can be returned.

Suspension

Suspension is classified into three:

A. Ordinary Suspension - where the pupil/student prohibited from attending regular classes, but is expected to report at the Prefect of Discipline's Office.

B. Working Suspension - students are required to do some manual works inside the school premises.

C. Preventive Suspension - where the pupil/student is prohibited totally to attend regular classes or entering the school campus.

Note: The school reserves the right to give your child the following additional sanctions (Depending on the degree of the offense committed)

All Offenses/Violations under category 3 and 4 or repeated violations of offenses under Category 2 will be sanctioned accordingly as follows;

Excluded from joining the following school activities such as FIELD TRIPS, JS PROM, GRADUATION MARCH, RECOGNITION DAY RITES and other school functions, and barred from holding an official position/membership of any of the school organizations (SBO, CLUBS, VARSITY TEAMS, CAT - I OFFICERS CORPS)

Probation

Academic Probation (AP)

A pupil/student is placed on Academic Probation the following year, if s/he incurs one final failing subject grade in any academic subject. Grades 74 and below are considered failing.

Special Academic Probation (SAP)

A pupil/student is place on Special academic Probation the following conditions:

    1. S/he incurs two (2) final failing subject grades in any academic subject.
    2. S/he was placed on Academic Probation the previous school year and has incurred another failing final grade in any academic subject in current school year.

An Academic Probation (AP and SAP) status is lifted at the end of the school year, if the pupil/student does not incur any failing final grade in any academic subject.


Retention

A pupil/student will be retained in the grade/year level when he/she incurs three (3) or more failing final subject grades.
A pupil/student will be retained if he/she incurs three or more failing final grade while on Academic Probation.
Should a pupil/student under retention status incur a failing final grade in any subject in the succeeding year he/she will be withdrawn from St. Francis Academy.


Withdrawal From School

A pupil/student is advised to withdraw from the school at the end of the school year for any of the following reasons:

    He/she is on SAP and has incurred a failing final grade in any academic subject.

    He/she is under RETENTION status, and has incurred a final failing grade in any academic subject.

    He/she failed Kinder 1 or 2. (Due to age limit, repetition in these two levels is not allowed).

    He/she is Special Deportment Probation in Tardiness (SDPT) and has incurred a grade of NI or NSI in Conduct in any quarter during the school year.


Deportment Probation in Conduct (SPC)

DPC is determined by any of the following criteria:

    A grade of NI in Conduct incurred twice or more during school year.

    A grade of NSA in Conduct twice or more during the school year. This may cause the pupil/student to be withdrawn from the school or to be placed on DPC, depending on the gravity of his/her offenses.


Special Deportment Probation in Conduct (SDPC)

SPDC is the last chance given to any pupil/student, who are having gone through a year of probation, does not improve his/her performance and behaviour.


Lifting of Deportment Probation in Conduct

The deportment Probation status (DPC or SDPC) is lifted if a pupil/student does not incur an NI or an NSA in any quarter of the school year.
