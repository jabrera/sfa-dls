Saint John Baptist De La Salle


Saint John Baptist de La Salle

Born in an aristocratic family in Rheims, France in 1651, St. John Baptist De La Salle surrendered high scholastic and ecclesiastical position and family wealth to work for the Christian education of the youth of his day by establishing an institute of religious teachers, St. La Salle popularized the use of the vernacular and similar methods in his schools. This founder of the La Salle Brothers dies in 1719 was canonized in 1900 and proclaimed Patron of all Teachers in 1950.

La Salle Brothers in the Philippines

The first group of Brothers, nine in number, arrived in Manila in 1911 on the invitation of the Archbishop of Manila, to establish a college. Paco was the original site of the Brothers' first school. Later in 1921, however, a new building was erected at the present site on Taft Avenue.

From this modest beginning, the pioneering efforts of the first La Salle Brothers have achieved tremendous results. Presently, there are La Salle schools in Iligan, Bacolod, Lipa, Alabang, Antipolo, Mandaluyong, Cavite and Manila, aside from the other De La Salle University supervised and La Salle Green hills - Supervised rural and city schools, where St. Francis Academy also belongs.

"If we can reach the boundaries, the world will become a brighter place, closer to peace, closer to human fraternity."
- Saint John Baptist De La Salle
